Introduction to Maze Wars

You are a giant, floating, killer eyeball trapped in a maze with other killer eyeballs. The goal of Maze Wars is to shoot everyone else you find in the maze, before they shoot you. There are no weapons, but you can shoot. You just can. Yes, the bullets are invisible, but they're still lethal – every hit is fatal. Killed players reappear at a random point in the maze. Play continues until someone reaches a score of ?? points. Here and there in the maze are places with a blue circle on the floor. Those are teleports. Step on them and you're instantly, randomly relocated. They can get you out of trouble, or into it. You never know.

Use the keys shown below to move and shoot. (The letter keys represent the original key mappings of the circa 1987 Macintosh version of MazeWars. The arrow keys and keypad mappings are new to this version.) As an experiment, you can also move by clicking on the walls and floor, or by using a scrollwheel, and you can shoot by clicking on your target. However, using the keyboard is almost always faster than using the mouse.

Note: If the keys don't seem to work, the browser is probably sending the keystrokes to its address bar. Click somewhere on the maze to correct that.

The maze dimensions are authentic, but the layout is not – it is randomly generated for each game. Also new to the game is that there will always be at least ?? players, even if they're all bots. As humans enter the game, the bots with the highest scores drop out. If humans exit, and the number of players falls below ??, new bots enter the game to keep it interesting. The bots aren't smart or particularly fast, but they are thorough, so they can keep you on your toes. To exit the game, just close your browser window. Within a few minutes your eyeball will be automatically removed from the maze.
